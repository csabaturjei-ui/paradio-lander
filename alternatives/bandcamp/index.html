What PAR Does That Bandcamp Structurally Can't

Being a company means being acquirable — PAR is a protocol, not a company

🌐

Decentralized Storage

Bandcamp's servers belong to whoever owns the company this week. When Epic sold to Songtradr, artists had no say and no guarantee. PAR stores tracks on IPFS — the community pins and maintains them. No board can vote to shut it down, no acquirer inherits control of your catalog.

⛓️

Zero Middlemen

Bandcamp takes 15% (dropping to 10% only after you cross $5,000 in sales) then routes the rest through Stripe and bank rails with a 48–72 hour delay. PAR's Solana smart contracts settle instantly to any wallet, globally, with no intermediary — and 0% platform cut from the first sale to the millionth.

📡

Community Curation

Bandcamp discovery relies on followers, genre tags, and editorial features that depend on a living, funded editorial team — a team that was heavily laid off after the Songtradr acquisition. PAR's curation is protocol-level: listeners pin what they love, the most-pinned tracks rise, and no editorial decision can be zeroed out by a cost-cutting round.
